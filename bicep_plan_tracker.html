Bicep Exercises
01
Standard Dumbbell Curl
Primary bicep builder · Sessions A & C
Foundation
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Stand upright, feet shoulder-width apart, one dumbbell in each hand.
  • Keep elbows pinned to your sides throughout the whole movement.
  • Curl both dumbbells up, rotating palms to face your shoulders at the top.
  • Squeeze your biceps hard at the top for 1 second.
  • Lower slowly — 2–3 seconds back to the start position.

Tempo: 2-1-2-1 — 2 seconds up, hold 1 second at the top, 2 seconds down, brief pause at the bottom before the next rep.
02
Hammer Curl
Brachialis & forearm · Sessions A & C
Foundation
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Hold dumbbells with a neutral grip — palms facing each other, like holding a hammer.
  • Keep elbows locked at your sides the entire time.
  • Curl straight up without rotating your wrists at all.
  • Lower slowly and controlled back to start.

Why it matters: Builds outer bicep and forearm thickness — gives your arm a fuller look.
03
Concentration Curl
Peak isolation · Sessions A & C
Isolation
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Sit on a chair, feet flat. Hold one dumbbell in your right hand.
  • Rest your right elbow on the inside of your right thigh.
  • Let the dumbbell hang down, then curl it up toward your shoulder.
  • Squeeze at the top. Lower slowly. Complete all reps, then switch arms.

Why it matters: Locks your elbow so you can't cheat — pure bicep isolation that builds the peak.
04
Slow Eccentric Curl
Tempo: 2s up · 1s hold · 4s down · Sessions B & C
Control
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Same setup as a standard curl.
  • Curl up in 2 seconds, squeeze and hold at the top for 1 second.
  • Lower the weight very slowly — count 4 full seconds going down.
  • Pause briefly at the bottom before starting the next rep.
  • Fight the descent the whole way. Never let gravity take over.

Tempo: 2-1-4-1 — This is the key difference from a standard curl. The 4-second lowering phase maximises time under tension and creates significantly more muscle growth stimulus.
05
Incline Dumbbell Curl
Long head stretch · Session B
Stretch
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Sit back on an inclined chair or sofa at roughly 45–60°.
  • Let your arms hang straight down behind your body — feel the deep stretch.
  • Curl both dumbbells up slowly from that fully stretched position.
  • Lower back down slowly. Do not swing forward off the seat.

Why it matters: Deeply stretches the long head of the bicep — builds the visible peak when you flex.
06
Cross-Body Hammer Curl
Brachialis depth · Session B
Control
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Hold dumbbells with a neutral grip (palms facing in).
  • Curl one dumbbell across your body toward the opposite shoulder.
  • Alternate arms each rep. Keep your elbow close to your torso throughout.

Why it matters: Develops the brachialis (muscle beneath the bicep), which pushes the bicep up, making your arm look bigger.
Push-Up Exercises
01
Standard Push-Up
Chest, shoulders, triceps · Push-Up Days 1 & 2
Core Move
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, fingers pointing forward.
  • Straight line from head to heels — no sagging hips or raised butt.
  • Lower your chest to just above the floor.
  • Push back up explosively and lock out your arms at the top.

If too hard: Drop to knee push-ups until you can do 10 clean full reps.
02
Wide Grip Push-Up
Chest focus · Push-Up Day 1
Variation
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Place hands about 6–8 inches wider than your shoulders.
  • Lower your chest with elbows flaring out to the sides.
  • Push back up. Wider grip = more chest activation.
03
Knee Push-Up
Beginner modifier · Push-Up Day 1
Beginner
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Get into push-up position, then lower your knees to the floor.
  • Keep your back straight — don't let your hips sag or rise.
  • Perform the push-up movement from the knees.

Goal: Use until you can do 10 clean standard push-ups in a row, then remove them.
04
Diamond Push-Up
Triceps · Push-Up Day 2
Advanced
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Place both hands together under your chest, forming a diamond shape with thumbs and index fingers.
  • Keep elbows close to your body as you lower down.
  • Push back up. Harder than standard — start with fewer reps.

Why it matters: Triceps make up 2/3 of your upper arm. Critical for overall arm size.
05
Incline Push-Up
Lower chest · Push-Up Day 2
Variation
▶ YouTube
▼
How to do it:
  • Place hands on a raised surface — chair, couch armrest, or table edge.
  • Body at a downward angle, feet flat on the floor.
  • Perform the push-up as normal. Incline targets the lower chest.

Form Rules
🔒
Pin Your Elbows
Keep elbows locked at your sides during all curls. Drifting elbows reduce bicep activation and strain the shoulder.
📏
Full Range of Motion
Lower the weight all the way down every rep. Partial reps build partial muscles. Full extension equals full growth.
🐢
Control the Descent
Take 2–3 seconds on the way down. Never drop the weight — the lowering phase is where most growth happens.
No Swinging
If you're swinging your back or hips to lift, the weight is too heavy. Drop 2–5 lbs and do it properly.
